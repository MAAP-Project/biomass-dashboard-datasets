Paraguay is a landlocked South American country that is divided by the Río Paraguay into two distinct geographic regions. The east (Paraguay Oriental) contains humid Atlantic, Chaco, and Cerrado forests, while the west (Paraguay Occidental) is primarily dry and humid Chaco. The Gran Chaco covers over 60 percent of Paraguay’s land and is South America’s second largest forest after the Amazon. In 2014 and 2015 Paraguay deployed their first national forest inventory (NFI) as part of their REDD+ program. Paraguay is now seeking to update their NFI and expand to regions that were unable to be surveyed due to inaccessibility in the vast forests.

Remote sensing data offers an attractive alternative to field surveys since it is freely accessible, spatially explicit, and can be used to model forest characteristics such as aboveground biomass. A collaborative team involving members of Paraguay’s National Forestry Institute, USGS SilvaCarbon, the CEOS Harmonization group, and US Forest Service is exploring a remote sensing approach to national biomass estimation. The approach integrates Paraguay’s NFI with GEDI LiDAR data and a Landsat canopy cover model to create a statistically viable estimate for Paraguay’s mean forest biomass. Importantly, the model can quantify uncertainties and be used to calculate a confidence interval around the estimate of mean biomass.

Biomass is estimated with a model that relates field measurements to remote sensing data. NFI data is matched to spatially overlapping GEDI waveforms to form a model building dataset, and all plots without colocated GEDI shot are excluded.

A map of percent canopy cover is also used in the biomass model, which we created for 2019 in order to be consistent with the first GEDI observations.

View the percentage tree cover for 2019

A regression model is used to predict field biomass using the LiDAR and canopy cover data.

The model can then be applied to all GEDI footprints in the country to create a national estimate of biomass and uncertainty. The national estimate can also be decomposed to subdomains, such as by forest types or within 6 km tiles.

View the estimated biomass in Paraguay within 6km grids

Our national estimate of biomass is 66.01 Mg per hectare (± ## 95% confidence interval), which is with the 69.89 Mg/ha reported by Paraguay to the FAO in 2020. Our generalizable approach can be applied anywhere with forests and GEDI observations. The field data used for model calibration does not need to be randomly distributed, making it suitable for countries without a well-established forest inventory.

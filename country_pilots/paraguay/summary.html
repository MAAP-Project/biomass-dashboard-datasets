Paraguay is a landlocked South American country that contains vast forest ecosystems. The Gran Chaco covers over 60 percent of Paraguay’s land and is South America’s second largest forest after the Amazon. The carbon stored in Paraguay’s forests was measured as part of the national REDD+ program, when the first national forest inventory (NFI) was deployed from 2014 to 2016. Paraguay is now seeking to update the NFI and expand to regions that were unreachable in the original survey. Remote sensing data offers an attractive alternative to field surveys since it is freely accessible, spatially explicit, and can be used to measure forest characteristics in areas that are inaccessible. A collaborative team involving members of Paraguay’s Instituto Forestal Nacional, USGS SilvaCarbon, the CEOS Biomass Harmonization group, and US Forest Service is exploring a remote sensing approach to update and supplement national biomass estimation.

The map shown to the left reflects NFI-calibrated interpretations of biomass at the locations of millions of measurements from NASA’s GEDI (Global Ecosystem Dynamics Investigation) lidar instrument, which is mounted on the International Space Station.
